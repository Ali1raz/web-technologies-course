Saber-Toothed Tiger

The Saber-Toothed Tiger, or Smilodon, was a prehistoric predator known for its enormous curved canine teeth. It roamed the Americas during the Ice Age, and although it's called a "tiger," it's not closely related to modern tigers.

Woolly Mammoth

The Woolly Mammoth was a large, elephant-like mammal covered in thick fur to survive the cold Ice Age climate. It had long, curved tusks and lived in northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Dodo

Archaeopteryx is known as the "first bird" and represents a crucial link in the evolution of birds from dinosaurs. It had feathers and wings like modern birds, but also dinosaur traits like teeth and a long bony tail. Fossils have been found in Germany and show it lived about 150 million years ago.
